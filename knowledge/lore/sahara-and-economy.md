# Sahara, The Architechs & Shopping District Economics

## The Architechs

**The Architechs** (originally called "Build Stone") were a group formed during **Season 6** consisting of **Grian**, **MumboJumbo**, and **Iskall85**. Originally conceived by Grian as a building collective, the group's ambition quickly escalated into one of the server's most legendary projects.

- Established in MumboJumbo's Season 6, Episode 5 and Grian's Season 6, Episode 4
- Each member had a role: Grian handled building aesthetics, Iskall handled redstone, Mumbo also handled complex redstone
- Built the **Sahara** mega-shop — their defining achievement

---

## Sahara: Hermitcraft's Amazon

**Sahara** was a fully automated, player-run mega-shop built by the Architechs in **Season 6/7**, located between the Shopping District and the Futuristic District.

### Concept
Described at launch as *"Amazon Prime in Minecraft, but faster"* — Sahara aimed to sell the **majority of in-game items in one place**, all automated.

### Technical Scale
- Required **at least 3 months of work** before its beta release
- Featured a massive **warehouse** storing all stock, connected via **redstone decoders** and **item filters** that processed customer orders
- Orders were delivered to the shopfront via **ice ducts** (high-speed item transport tunnels)
- Considered **one of the biggest projects in Hermitcraft history** at the time

### The Spin-Offs
- **Sahara Fuel** (Iskall85): A chain of "gas stations" selling XP, rockets, and golden carrots
- **Sahara Now**: A faster-delivery variant — reportedly *more profitable* than the original Sahara

### The Profit: 5 Diamond Blocks
Despite the enormous scale, by the end of Season 6 the Architechs had only **broken even**, netting a total profit of **5 diamond blocks**. This became a legendary punchline — years of work for essentially nothing. The 5 diamond blocks were launched toward MumboJumbo's base at the start of Season 7, where they landed as the final remnant of the operation.

## Head Games (Season 7)

**Head Games** was a player-vs-player economy game hosted by **ZombieCleo** in Season 7.

### Rules
- Players and mob **heads** had point values
- Heads could only be obtained via PvP from signed-up Hermits
- Each day, a random Hermit's head was worth **double points**
- The player with the most points at the end won **288 diamonds**
- Game ran from sign-up until **April 10, 2020**

Head Games was a way to inject PvP and economic competition into the Shopping District without disrupting the broader server.

## Shopping District Economics

### Season 6
- First major shopping district on a mushroom island (clean terrain, no mob spawns)
- Established the template of player-run shops with diamond currency

### Season 7 (Cowmercial District)
- Located at **the exact centre of the world** on a mushroom island
- Nicknamed the **"Cowmercial District"** by Rendog and Xisumavoid (mooshrooms = cows = commerce)
- Iskall85 proposed: **1 diamond block per 100 blocks of horizontal area** (10×10) — a space tax to prevent shops sprawling forever. Universally accepted and became a rule.

### Season 8
- No centralised shopping district — shops were scattered across the world near Hermit bases
- A deliberate experiment in distributed commerce

### Season 10 — Hermit Permits
Grian proposed a **Hermit Permit system** — a pass granting the right to sell a specific category of item in the Shopping District. Key details:
- All Hermits needed a permit to sell
- Permit distribution was determined by **Demise results** (the last-Hermit-standing game)
- **FalseSymmetry** won Demise and received a **Joker Permit** — the right to sell any item
- Created a novel intersection between a survival game and shopping district governance

### Season 11
- **GoodTimesWithScar** was tasked with building the entire Season 11 Shopping District from scratch

## Diamond as Currency

Throughout Hermitcraft's history, **diamonds (and diamond blocks)** serve as the server's primary currency. Key denominations:
- **1 diamond** = basic unit
- **1 diamond block** = 9 diamonds = medium value
- **Double chest of diamond blocks** = extreme wealth/prestige

The economy is entirely player-run — no mods or plugins force pricing. Supply, demand, and social reputation drive all commerce.
