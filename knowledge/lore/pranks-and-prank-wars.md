# Notable Pranks & Prank Wars

## Overview

Pranking is a core part of Hermitcraft culture. Unlike competitive servers, pranks on Hermitcraft are collaborative entertainment — they are elaborately planned, executed with craft, and always done with the intention of making *both* the victim and the audience laugh. **Grian** is the most prominent prankster, but many Hermits have legendary prank moments.

---

## Season 6 Prank War

The Season 6 Prank War escalated into the full **Civil War** (see `civil-war-season6.md`), but several individual pranks stand out:

### Iskall85's Chicken Statue
Iskall built a giant **chicken in a Grian costume** (referencing Grian's alter-ego "Poultry Man") atop Grian's base, with diorite used to simulate bird droppings. This single prank inadvertently started the entire Civil War by being blamed on Rendog.

### MumboJumbo's Obsidian Trial
Mumbo pranked Grian and Iskall85 with a fake "gifting ceremony" — presenting them items in shulker boxes, then surrounding them with obsidian that they had to break **without a pickaxe** (as Mumbo had once been forced to do). The idea was to make them experience what he'd endured.

### Grian's AFK Prank on Mumbo
Grian attempted to prank Mumbo while he was AFK by giving him a riptide trident — and the results were chaotic. Referenced by Grian on Twitter: *"Hermitcraft 6: Episode 30 - MUMBO AFK PRANK!"*

## Season 7 Pranks

### The Poultry Man / Hot Guy Chicken Flood
Grian (as "Poultry Man") and Scar (as "Hot Guy") teamed up and built a machine that **filled Docm77's perimeter with chickens**. Doc was furious — it took many hours to clean up — and he relocated a large number of chickens directly into Grian's base as retaliation.

### The Mycelium Resistance Pranks
The entire Season 7 Turf War was punctuated by pranks on both sides. The Resistance planted mycelium patches covertly; HEP deployed wolves to destroy the sheep spreading it. Each escalation felt like an elaborate prank.

## Season 9 Prank War

### Docm77's Tunnel Bore Explosion
The Season 9 Prank War started when **Grian and GoodTimesWithScar** (as their alter-egos Poultry Man and Hot Guy) blew up **Docm77's tunnel bore** — a major piece of infrastructure he was using to excavate his perimeter.

### Covering Doc's Perimeter
While Docm77 was on holiday, **the Buttercups** (Grian, Mumbo, Scar) — joined by many other Hermits after the idea was pitched at a weekly Hermitcraft meeting — undertook the enormous task of **completely filling in Doc's 500×500 block perimeter** with terraformed land. The result was a sprawling plains landscape complete with trees and flowers, covering all of Doc's carefully cleared work.

Doc's reaction upon return was a highlight of Season 9.

## Notable One-Off Pranks Across Seasons

### Etho's Invisible Glass Subscription (Multiple Seasons)
Etho created a **free glass raffle** — but the "free glass" was **light gray stained glass panes** (invisible in the standard inventory UI) distributed across the empty slots of storage chests belonging to Hermits all over the server. He then launched a paid **unsubscription service** so players could pay to *stop* receiving free invisible glass. One of the most technically elegant and conceptually clever pranks in server history.

### FalseSymmetry's Sand & Cactus Debut (Season 2)
False's very first act on the Hermitcraft server was to fill the bases of **KingDaddyDMAC** and **Hypnotizd** with sand and cactus. A memorable debut that set the tone for her playful personality.

### Head-Based Pranks
Throughout multiple seasons, Hermits have used player head blocks (which look identical to the player's skin) to create elaborate "dummy" versions of themselves or others — hiding among statues, planting decoys, or creating scenes that confuse and alarm other players.

---

## Grian's Notable Prank Repertoire

Grian is the server's most prolific prankster. Across seasons, his signature moves include:
- **AFK ambushes** — catching players while logged off
- **Head-statue deception** — using his own head placed at strategic locations
- **Large-scale transformations** — converting entire areas while someone is absent
- **Double-agent schemes** — the Civil War double-agent plot
- **Elaborate traps** with fake rewards
